CONCEPT 02 // NIGHT-FLIGHT MISSION CONTROL

Orbit.

The world seen from a satellite at 2am — Charlie’s travel record rendered as live telemetry in a stats-lover’s cockpit.

THE VIBE Deep-space navy, a glowing dot-matrix Earth, mono numerals everywhere. Every page reads like an instrument panel: readouts, not decorations. Visited countries light up like city lights on a night flight; the rest of the planet waits in the dark, quietly tempting you.

WHY IT FITS THE RULES Telemetry is transparency. Each score is displayed with its inputs right beside it — distance, tourists, area — annotated in plain English so a 15-year-old can audit the math. And on a dot-matrix Earth every country gets its dots: Nauru glows exactly as brightly as Brazil.

WHAT CHANGES VS TODAY Today’s UI is plain light Tailwind. Orbit flips to a dark HUD: Space Grotesk display type, JetBrains Mono for all numerals and microlabels, corner-bracket panels, a status line pinned to your home base, and stat tiles rebuilt as instrument readouts with thin progress bars.

What to notice

  • Dot-matrix Earth — the signature image. Visited countries glow like night-flight city lights; the dark dots are the to-do list.
  • Tooltips are adventure bait: hover any dark country and it tells you what it’s worth. Mongolia teases “up to 186.6” — go get it.
  • Instrument tiles: label, big mono number, 6px progress bar. Total Points is the hero readout in signal cyan.
  • Home base status line pins the whole page to real coordinates — every score on the site is distance-from-London.
  • Great-circle flight paths are decoration at 30% opacity — they never encode data, the dots do.

What to notice

  • Questions stay human — “How far is it?” in Space Grotesk — while the answers carry mono data chips a stats-lover can audit.
  • The flight-computer line shows the whole formula in one glance: BASE = 4.4 × (6.94 + 5.16) = 53.2. Nothing hidden.
  • Two thin bars separate “score progress” from “exploration” — 62% of Japan’s max, 36% of the bonus. Different questions, different bars.
  • The prefecture map is a live checklist: lit = visited, dark = still to explore, and the list beside it prices every next trip.
  • Every readout carries its unit and source microlabel — Tier 2, P85, geo/JP.json — grounded in real data.

What to notice

  • The tug-of-war is a signal-strength bar: center hairline, mono readouts at each end, a cyan marker showing exactly where the balance sits.
  • Four-state map legend — You / Maya / Contested / Unclaimed — always visible; the colors never appear anywhere else in the UI.
  • Contested countries get blinking-cursor tags in amber — unresolved signals, waiting for someone to log a province.
  • The reassurance line is part of the design: battles are a lens on your points, never a change to them.

Map data provenance

All maps in this document were generated from the app’s real geodata: the world-atlas 110m TopoJSON used by react-simple-maps (Equal Earth projection, 20° graticule) and client/public/geo/JP.json for the 47 prefectures. Scores shown are live engine output from server/src/lib/points.js with home = GB.

Charts follow house rules: numerals in ink (never series color), thin bars ≤10px with rounded data ends, font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums, visible legends on every multi-color map, glow as decoration only.

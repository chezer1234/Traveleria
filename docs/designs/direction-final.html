From Atlas — the whole visual system

  • Warm paper, deep ink, gold-leaf rules, Fraunces + Inter
  • Maps as numbered plates with cartouches, graticules, compass roses
  • The Reckoning — score breakdown as a printed ledger
  • Passport stamps for visits, stats as ledger entries

From Jetstream — the trophies & chart energy

  • The trophy cabinet: ~10 achievements, earned or chased
  • Milestone ticks on progress bars — the next goal is always visible
  • The “36% explored” pill riding the bar end
  • Big, confident numerals on the tug-of-war

Merged carefully

  • One chart language across every page — see Nº 02 below
  • Gold leaf gilds earned trophies; it still never encodes chart data
  • Values stay in ink, legends stay visible, marks stay thin
  • Portrait phone is a first-class layout — see Nº 03 below

What this means: all four pages — Map, Country, Battle, and the new Trophies — ship in the Atlas skin.

What to notice

  • Earned trophies are gilded — gold-leaf medallions with ink engraving; the Scout / Ranger / Legend ladder steps through bronze, silver, gold. Locked trophies are blind-embossed paper outlines: present, waiting, never hidden.
  • Locked trophies carry a thin ink progress bar — Experience Collector shows 2 of 5, and Hard Mode taunts with real engine data: Turkmenistan, 1 tourist per 402 residents.
  • Every inscription is dated and traceable — the 10,000 km Club cites New Zealand at 18,814 km, straight from the same haversine the scoring engine uses.
  • Gold gilds achievements, but still never encodes chart data — the cabinet is the one place gold means something, and it means “done”.
  • Trophy thresholds are examples for Charlie to tune — the ten shown here (experiences, sub-region ladder, distance, difficulty, points) are a starting set, not a spec.

Responsive rules

  • Single column below 640 px — panels, plates and the Reckoning stack; stat tiles fall into a 2-col grid.
  • Nav collapses to a hamburger (existing behaviour) — wordmark left, page name centre, menu right.
  • Map plates keep their aspect ratio full-bleed to the screen edge, with the legend below and a tap-to-zoom affordance.
  • Ledger rows wrap question-above-answer — the value chip stays right-aligned on the question line, the plain-English reason wraps beneath.
  • Minimum 44 px touch targets on every checkbox, toggle and the hamburger — engraved does not mean fiddly.

What to notice

  • Nothing is redesigned for mobile — it reflows. The same ledger tiles, Reckoning rows and medallions, one column narrower.
  • Total Points becomes a sticky footer bar on the map page — the hero stat never scrolls away.

Next steps

From decision to shipped feature — per the CLAUDE.md workflow
  1. Write the feature doc — docs/features/visual-refresh-atlas.md: scope, trophy definitions and thresholds, open questions for Charlie (to be written before any code).
  2. Branch feature/visual-refresh-atlas, then implement in order: tokens → Map → Country → Battle → Trophies — the token layer first so every page restyles from one place.
  3. Q&A the trophy thresholds with Charlie — the ten trophies and their numbers in this document are examples to tune, not a spec.
  4. Test in Chrome at 1280 and 390 px, keep CI green, PR with a link back to the feature doc, then update docs/points-system.md if trophy rules touch scoring copy.

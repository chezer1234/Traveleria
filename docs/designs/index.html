Charlie picked Atlas — with Jetstream's trophy cabinet.

The Atlas system ships everywhere, plus a ten-trophy cabinet, one seamless chart language merged from Atlas and Jetstream, and portrait-phone layouts. The concepts below are kept for the record.

Atlas
CONCEPT 01

Atlas

An heirloom expedition atlas. Warm paper, engraved serifs, passport stamps and great-circle routes — every trip inscribed into a book of maps that feels like it belongs in a museum, except it's yours.

Signature move: the score breakdown as a printed ledger — “The Reckoning” — with the arithmetic set like a sum in a 19th-century survey.
View Atlas →
ORBIT
CONCEPT 02

Orbit

Night-flight mission control. A glowing dot-matrix Earth on deep navy, monospace telemetry, instrument-panel stat tiles. The world seen from a satellite at 2am — built for the stats obsessive.

Signature move: the dot-grid world map — 1,624 dots, one per ~81,000 km² of land — that lights up as you travel.
View Orbit →
Jetstream
CONCEPT 03

Jetstream

Bold travel-game energy. Boarding-pass country pages, XP bars with milestone ticks, achievement stickers, and battles staged like a broadcast football fixture. Points as a sport you want to win.

Signature move: the country header as a boarding pass — perforation, barcode, and your score printed on the stub.

Grounded in the real app

  • Every number is real output from server/src/lib/points.js with home = UK: Japan base 53.2, explorer ceiling 84.6, max 137.8; Mongolia max 186.6.
  • World geometry is the app's own world-atlas TopoJSON; Japan's 47 prefectures come from client/public/geo/JP.json.
  • The mock traveller has the same 23 countries in every concept, so comparisons are fair.

Accessible by construction

  • All three battle palettes (You / Opponent / Contested) were validated for colour-vision-deficiency separation and contrast against each concept's surface — including the current blue/red/purple, which passes.
  • Every multi-colour map ships a visible legend; values are always printed in text colour, never only encoded in a hue.
  • Each concept ends with implementation notes: Tailwind v4 @theme tokens and the Google Fonts to load.
